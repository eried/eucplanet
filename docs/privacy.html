Privacy Policy

EUC Planet Android app · Last updated 2026-06-04

EUC Planet is an open-source companion app for electric unicycles. It is published and maintained by Erwin Ried. This page describes what the app accesses on your device and what happens to that data.

Short version

  • The app does not have a server. It runs entirely on your phone.
  • It does not send your data to the developer or to any third party.
  • It does not contain advertising, analytics, or tracking SDKs.
  • Trip recordings, settings, and telemetry stay on your device unless you explicitly choose to sync them to a cloud folder you own (via Android's Storage Access Framework).

What the app accesses

  • Bluetooth:to scan for and connect to your wheel.
  • Precise location (GPS):only while a trip is being recorded or while the Recording screen is open, so the recorded CSV contains coordinates, altitude, and GPS speed. Location is never used for advertising, identification, or sent to any server by the app.
  • Notifications:to show the ongoing connection notification and deliver alarm alerts.
  • Foreground service:to keep the BLE connection and GPS tracking alive while the screen is off during a ride.
  • Camera + microphone (opt-in):only when you open Overlay Studio and start recording. The Studio is a self-contained video / photo recorder for capturing rides with a live telemetry overlay; the captured files land in your own device storage and the app never streams them anywhere.
  • Storage access (optional):if you enable cloud sync, you pick a folder yourself through Android's file picker (Google Drive, OneDrive, Dropbox, local, etc.). The app only reads/writes inside that folder. You can revoke access at any time through Android settings.

What is collected or stored

  • On your device: your app settings, the Bluetooth MAC address of the last connected wheel (so auto-connect works), and any trip CSV files you record.
  • In your cloud folder (optional, only if you enable sync): a copy of your app settings and your trip files. This folder belongs to you; the developer has no access to it.

What is not collected

  • No account, no login, no email, no phone number.
  • No analytics events, crash reports, or usage metrics sent off-device.
  • No advertising identifiers.
  • No contact list, call log, SMS, or photo-library access.
  • The camera and microphone are only ever opened from inside Overlay Studio while you have its recording screen open. Footage stays on your device.

Permissions explained

  • BLUETOOTH_SCAN, BLUETOOTH_CONNECT:required to find and control the wheel. The scan is flagged neverForLocation.
  • ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION, ACCESS_COARSE_LOCATION:only used to record your position while a trip is being recorded.
  • FOREGROUND_SERVICE, FOREGROUND_SERVICE_CONNECTED_DEVICE, FOREGROUND_SERVICE_LOCATION:to keep the wheel connection and GPS running in the background while you ride.
  • CAMERA, RECORD_AUDIO:only used by Overlay Studio while you have its recording screen open. Both prompts appear the first time you tap the Studio's record button and can be revoked at any time in Android Settings.
  • POST_NOTIFICATIONS:to show the connection status and alerts.
  • VIBRATE:for alarm haptic feedback.
  • INTERNET:used only when you explicitly upload trip files to your chosen cloud provider through the Android sync stack, and when the in-app Navigator queries the OSRM routing endpoint to solve a turn-by-turn route. Routing requests contain only the start + stop coordinates you're solving for; no identifiers are attached.

Data retention and deletion

All data is local. Uninstalling the app removes everything stored in the app's private data directory. Trip files that you exported or synced to your own cloud folder remain under your control.

Children

The app is not directed at children under 13.

Open source

The full source code is public at github.com/eried/eucplanet, so the claims above can be independently verified.

About this website

This policy covers the Android app. The marketing website at eucplanet.ried.no loads Microsoft Clarity so the developer can see which pages and links people actually use. Clarity records page visits, clicks, and anonymised session replays. It does not run inside the app and has no access to your wheel, your rides, or your device data. You can opt out with any standard tracker blocker.
